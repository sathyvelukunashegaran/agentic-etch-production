# States and responsive composition

## Responsive priorities

Record what must remain visible, adjacent, ordered, and actionable at each target width. Preserve proof near the claim it supports and keep the primary action reachable.

Review at minimum the project's narrow, tablet, desktop, and wide targets plus stress widths around major reflow points.

## Input modes

Account for keyboard, touch, pointer, zoom, and reduced motion. Hover cannot be the only way to reveal essential behavior.

## State matrix

For each interactive or data-driven component consider:

- default, hover, focus-visible, active, and disabled;
- loading, success, error, and empty;
- long and short content;
- missing media or data;
- first use and returning use;
- offline or integration failure when relevant.

Responsive completion is a design obligation during implementation, not a final cleanup stage.

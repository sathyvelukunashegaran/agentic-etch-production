# Visual rules

## Typography

- Keep long-form body measure near 60–75 characters.
- Pair typefaces on a real contrast axis or use one family deliberately.
- Keep display tracking readable; avoid compressed letterforms that touch.
- Balance short headings and prevent overflow at every target width.
- Establish roles, not isolated font sizes.

## Color

- Choose a color strategy before choosing individual colors: restrained, committed, full palette, or color-drenched.
- Derive neutrals from the brand direction instead of defaulting to fashionable warm or cool tints.
- Verify text contrast in rendered states, including placeholder and disabled text.
- Use color to clarify hierarchy, state, or identity; decoration alone is insufficient.

## Layout

- Use varied but intentional spacing rhythm.
- Use flex for one-dimensional relationships and grid for genuinely two-dimensional composition.
- Define container, content, and full-bleed behavior explicitly.
- Use a semantic layering scale rather than arbitrary extreme z-index values.
- Make optical corrections where mathematical centering looks wrong.

## Motion

- Motion communicates hierarchy, continuity, feedback, or atmosphere.
- Prefer transform, opacity, filter, clip, or mask over layout-property animation.
- Provide reduced-motion behavior.
- Never hide essential content behind an animation trigger that may not fire.

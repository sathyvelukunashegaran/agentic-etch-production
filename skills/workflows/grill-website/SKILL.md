# Grill Website

Turn an unclear website initiative into a durable, approved contract.

## Load first

Explicitly load `website-strategy` and `proof-chain` before asking the first question. Read `CONTEXT.md`, `SITE-CONTRACT.md` and the evidence register when present.

## Interview discipline

- Ask one decision question at a time and provide a recommended answer.
- Explore the environment for facts; reserve questions for decisions.
- Challenge broad audiences, generic differentiation, undefined conversions and unsupported claims.
- Walk dependencies in order. Do not discuss page decoration while the page's job remains unresolved.
- Update `CONTEXT.md`, `SITE-CONTRACT.md` and `docs/site/evidence.md` as decisions crystallize rather than batching memory at the end.
- When discussion cannot resolve a decision, branch to research or a cheap prototype and return with the evidence.

## Required resolution

Resolve at least:

1. business outcome;
2. primary users and triggering situations;
3. offer and meaningful differentiation;
4. primary and secondary conversion paths;
5. information architecture and page jobs;
6. message hierarchy;
7. proof obligations and gaps;
8. visual register constraints;
9. functional, responsive and accessibility obligations;
10. explicit out-of-scope boundaries.

## Approval gate

Present the completed contract and strongest unresolved risk. Do not mark the contract approved until the human explicitly accepts it.

## Completion criterion

The session is complete when another fresh agent can read the artifacts and explain who the site serves, what each major page must accomplish, how conversion works, which claims are admissible and which decisions remain open.
